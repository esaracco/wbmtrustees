Managing ACLs for a path

  • Click on "Add" to add a new rule. First of all, tou must select the group or the user concerned by this rule. A rule may apply either to a group or to a user, but never to both.
    Then, specify the rights by selecting the relevant checkboxes. You may also specify, in addition to the rights, a "modifier". For instance, you can explicitly forbid reading by specifying "D" in before the "R" attribute.

    The meaning of the attributes and modifiers is as follows:

    • AttributeMeaning
    RReading files
    WWriting files and directories
    BGoing through directories
    EReading directories
    XFile execution
    UUse Unix permissions
    Modifiers
    CReinitialise persmissions (instead of defining them)
    DDeny (instead of allow)
    !The attribute applies to all but the specified owner (group or user)
    OOne level only. The attributes applies to the specified directory and to the files it contains, but not to sub-directories

    • The ACLs list defined for the path is displayed under the add form. You may modify any of the existing rules. What has been said about adding applies to modifying. In the left panel which is displayed for each line, you may delete a rule by clicking on the "Delete" button.

    • When you want to save your modifications, click on the "Save" button. You will then be redirected to the module's main page.
